visualisation.


The below shown data-driven visualisation, is a logarithmic map of the universe from Gott et al. (2005) at Princeton. It maps the unfathomable onto a sheet of paper; the scale is truly astounding.

Earth (and us) are at the bottom of the strip near the green area labeled "surface of the earth." The green dots are satellites. ISS is the International Space Station. As one moves up from there, one gets exponentially farther away from the Earth: moon, planets, Milky Way (red dots), other galaxies, and at the top, the "end" of the universe (the farthest visible point at the time big bang). The horizontal axis sweeps the night sky 360 degrees over 24 hours.
